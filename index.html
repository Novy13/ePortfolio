Nursing Philosophy Statement

Nursing Philosophy Statement

For the past year and a half as a nursing student, I always here the phrase “nursing is an art”. The very first time I heard it, I just laughed it off but as I progressed in nursing school, I start to understand this phrase little by little. This notion has profoundly shaped my understanding of the profession, guiding me to perceive nursing as a dynamic blend of science, empathy, and creativity. The core principle of holistic care, deeply embedded in my philosophy, aligns seamlessly with the artistic nature of nursing. One poignant experience that exemplifies this was; it was our duty in Medicine ward and I was caring for a patient whose physical symptoms improved, yet emotional distress persisted due to lack of family support. He was not from the highlands, he is from Manila so maybe it is also one of the reason why he does not have a watcher and that much visitors. It saddens me that he always tell me about that he’s son is visiting him today but as the time goes on, there is no signs of his son. So aside from taking his V/S and giving him medications, I made sure to stay by his bedside, making him feel that he is not alone. I also tried to engage in conversation with him as often as possible for him to be distracted and not think about his family. This further highlighted the intricate canvas of healthcare, where addressing not only physical ailments but also emotional and social aspects is essential. This realization underscored the artistic intuition required to craft personalized care plans that go beyond standardized protocols. Compassion, another fundamental aspect of my philosophy, takes center stage in the artistry of nursing. During challenging periods when caring for terminally ill patients, the importance of genuine empathy and emotional support became abundantly clear. These experiences reinforced my belief that compassion is not just a skill but an art, requiring an authentic connection with each individual's unique human experience. Evidence-based practice, a scientific cornerstone of nursing, also intertwines with the artistry of adapting interventions based on patient needs. Our clinical instructor had always made an emphasis on this one, that in everything we do it must always be evidence-based. So as a novice nurse, we always make sure to double check everything in our books or in our CI’s before administering or giving medications. Cultural competence and a commitment to health equity, integral components of my philosophy, find resonance in the artistic palette of nursing. As we all know, Benguet and Baguio consist of a very diverse ethnic groups and also the fact that people from different places or from the lowlands also come to our hospitals here for better treatment. So before interacting with our patients, we always put in our minds that we must always be sensible and sensitive. Engaging with patients from diverse backgrounds has been akin to exploring a rich tapestry of human experiences. These encounters have highlighted the importance of cultural sensitivity and advocacy for fair healthcare practices, contributing to the inclusive and equitable canvas of my nursing approach. Open communication, a vital thread in my philosophy, mirrors the art of dialogue in nursing. As we always include in our FDAR, established rapport. Easy to say and write but very difficult to achieve. I myself, also struggle in establishing rapport easily but I do hope that as I progress, I would be able to master this vital skill. Establishing transparent communication with patients and their families not only builds trust but also fosters collaboration. This collaborative approach, akin to the synergy between an artist and their subject, empowers patients to actively participate in decisions regarding their care. In summary, my nursing philosophy is a harmonious symphony of science and art, where evidence-based practice, compassion, cultural competence, and open communication blend seamlessly to create a canvas of holistic and personalized care. Like an artist refining their masterpiece, my philosophy continues to evolve through experiences, challenges, and a perpetual commitment to the ever-evolving artistry of nursing.

Clinical Skills: The Art and Science of Patient Care

 Handwashing, Patient’s positioning, Patient transfer from bed to stretcher, bed to wheelchair and vice versa. Bed making, occupied and unoccupied. Bed bath, Oral Care, Back Massage, Perennial Care, Shampooing, Tepid Sponge Bath.

 IM, ID and SubQ. IV insertion, Priming, IV push medications and oral. Charting and Documentation such as FDAR, I &O, NCP, TPR and such. Communication Proficiency: A Bridge to Holistic Care

 In a busy medical-surgical unit, I was assigned to care for a patient with complex needs and a history of non-compliance with medications. Recognizing the potential complications, I initiated open communication with the patient, addressing their concerns, clarifying the importance of adherence, and exploring any barriers to following the prescribed regimen.

 Establishing rapport is a fundamental skill in nursing, facilitating trust, communication, and a positive patient experience.  In some of our clinical rotations, I was assigned to care for a patient who arrived in distress, visibly anxious and reluctant to share details about their medical history. Recognizing the importance of building trust, I began by introducing myself, maintaining eye contact, and using open-ended questions to encourage the patient to express their concerns.

Leadership Community Service

One of our rotations were the RHU. Our clinical group was assigned on Barangay Bahong. On our three days duty there, we were able to meet different people with different personalities. Our group was tasked to have a house to house visit in the community. I would say that we were overwhelm by their way of accepting us student nurses. Some even offered food and drinks, we also helped some residents with their work while interviewing them and some were happy and even gave us some flowers. Unfortunately, we also encounter some household where they don’t want to entertain us or even close their doors just to avoid us. In one of our 3 day duty there, it was also there immunization and pre natal check up there, lucky for us, the nurses in their Barangay Health Clinic, allowed us to inject some vaccines and such. All in all our experience there was unforgettable, yes, it is indeed a tiring experience but a memorable one.

UHS

Our group were also deployed to UHS or University Health Services. Some of our batch mates who were deployed there before us, says that it is boring there and such. However, during our duty there, we had so many patients to the point that we had 78 patients in our first day. Shock was an understatement of what I felt at that moment because it is far from being boring. The following days, fortunately, the number of patients decreased but still, I was exhausted. Our rotation on UHS ended with us feeling tired but heart is so full with all the memories and learning's.

Health Teachings

During our first year, we did health teaching regarding the prevention of Dengue to grade 5 students of Bekkel Elementary School. It was fun interacting with the children and playing with them. I considered it as one of my core memories.

Blood Donation
